### 6. Cover Letter
Use this template:

> Dear Editor,
>
> I am pleased to submit the manuscript "Mechanistic Interpretability of Plant Genomic Foundation Models: Dissecting How Transformers Process Plant DNA" for consideration in NAR Genomics and Bioinformatics.
>
> This work presents the first comprehensive mechanistic interpretability analysis of a plant genomic foundation model. Using five complementary techniques—linear probing, attention analysis, activation patching, sparse autoencoders, and cross-species representation analysis—we dissect how Plant-DnaGemma (152M parameters) builds hierarchical representations of plant DNA features.
>
> Key contributions include:
> 1. Establishing a methodological framework for interpretability in genomic AI
> 2. Revealing emergent phylogenetic structure in learned representations 
> 3. Discovering biologically structured sparse features through autoencoder analysis
> 4. Demonstrating layer-wise specialization in genomic feature processing
>
> As foundation models become increasingly important in computational biology, understanding their internal mechanisms is critical for building trust and guiding model improvements. This work provides actionable insights for the genomics community and establishes interpretability techniques that can be applied to other genomic foundation models.
>
> All analysis code, trained model weights, and datasets are publicly available at https://github.com/Biodyn-AI/aido-mechinterp to ensure full reproducibility.
>
> We believe this work aligns perfectly with NAR Genomics and Bioinformatics' focus on large-scale data analysis and computational methods in genomics.
>
> Sincerely,
> Ihor Kendiukhov
