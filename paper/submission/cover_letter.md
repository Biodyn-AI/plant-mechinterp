# Cover Letter - NAR Genomics and Bioinformatics

Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript "Mechanistic Interpretability of Plant Genomic Foundation Models: Dissecting How Transformers Process Plant DNA" for consideration in NAR Genomics and Bioinformatics.

This work presents the first comprehensive mechanistic interpretability analysis of a plant genomic foundation model. Using five complementary techniques—linear probing, attention analysis, activation patching, sparse autoencoders, and cross-species representation analysis—we dissect how Plant-DnaGemma (152M parameters) builds hierarchical representations of plant DNA features.

## Key Contributions

1. **Establishing a methodological framework** for interpretability in genomic AI that can be applied to other foundation models
2. **Revealing emergent phylogenetic structure** in learned representations without explicit evolutionary supervision
3. **Discovering biologically structured sparse features** through autoencoder analysis (70.6% sparsity)
4. **Demonstrating layer-wise specialization** in genomic feature processing with peak performance at layer 10 (71.8% accuracy)

## Significance and Impact

As foundation models become increasingly important in computational biology, understanding their internal mechanisms is critical for:
- Building trust in AI predictions for genomic applications
- Guiding architectural improvements for plant-specific models  
- Identifying potential failure modes and biases
- Enabling more interpretable genomic AI systems

This work provides actionable insights for the genomics community and establishes interpretability techniques that can be applied broadly across genomic foundation models.

## Reproducibility and Open Science

All analysis code, trained sparse autoencoder weights, and datasets are publicly available at https://github.com/Biodyn-AI/plant-mechinterp to ensure full reproducibility. This aligns with NAR Genomics and Bioinformatics' strong emphasis on reproducible research.
